code: "F3 E1"
meaningshort: "Water level system error detected."
meaninglong: "The F3 E1 error indicates that the washer’s pressure sensor is unable to correctly read the water level inside the drum. Whirlpool front‑load washers use an electronic pressure transducer connected to a small air hose. As water fills the tub, air pressure increases in the hose, allowing the control board to determine the water level. If the sensor detects values outside the expected range, or if the hose is blocked, kinked, or leaking, the washer halts operation to prevent overfilling or underfilling. This is one of the most common Whirlpool front‑load error codes and often relates to debris in the pressure hose or a failing sensor."
symptoms:
  - "Washer stops early in the cycle"
  - "Washer fills and drains repeatedly"
  - "F3 E1 appears shortly after starting a cycle"
  - "Washer refuses to advance past the sensing stage"
  - "Unexpected draining during fill"
causes:
  - "Blocked or kinked pressure hose"
  - "Loose pressure hose connection at the tub or sensor"
  - "Faulty water level pressure sensor"
  - "Debris or soap residue clogging the air dome"
  - "Control board misreading sensor values"
stepfixes:
  - "Unplug the washer and remove the top panel."
  - "Inspect the pressure hose for kinks, cracks, or blockages."
  - "Ensure the hose is firmly connected at both ends."
  - "Blow gently through the hose to confirm it is clear."
  - "If the hose is clear, replace the pressure sensor."
  - "If the error persists, the main control board may require service."
safetynotes:

  - "Avoid using sharp tools near the pressure hose."
  - "Do not run the washer with the pressure hose disconnected."
