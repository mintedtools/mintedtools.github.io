code: "F3 E2"
meaningshort: "Temperature sensor (NTC) error."
meaninglong: "The F3 E2 error indicates that the washer’s temperature sensor, also known as the NTC thermistor, is reading values outside the expected range. Whirlpool front‑load washers use this sensor to monitor incoming water temperature and regulate heating cycles. If the sensor is open, shorted, or disconnected, the washer cannot safely control temperature and halts the cycle. This error is common when the sensor wiring becomes loose or when mineral buildup affects the sensor’s accuracy."
symptoms:
  - "Washer stops early in the cycle"
  - "Water temperature seems incorrect"
  - "F3 E2 appears shortly after filling"
  - "Washer refuses to start certain cycles"
causes:
  - "Faulty NTC temperature sensor"
  - "Loose or corroded wiring harness"
  - "Sensor shorted due to moisture"
  - "Open circuit in the sensor wiring"
  - "Control board misreading sensor values"
stepfixes:
  - "Unplug the washer and remove the rear panel."
  - "Inspect the temperature sensor wiring for damage."
  - "Ensure the sensor connector is fully seated."
  - "Test the sensor with a multimeter (typically 10–12kΩ at room temperature)."
  - "Replace the sensor if readings are out of range."
  - "If wiring and sensor are good, the control board may be faulty."
safetynotes:
  - "Disconnect power before accessing the heater or sensor."
